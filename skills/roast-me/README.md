# roast-me

**Code is cheap now. Judgment is not.**

`roast-me` is for work that sounds convincing, looks polished, and still deserves a harder review.

It turns "seems fine" into clear concerns, explicit reasoning, and fixes worth backing.

## Why teams use it

- It adds pressure early, while the work is still cheap to change.
- It turns "seems fine" into concrete judgment.
- It keeps the roast sharp enough to expose weak reasoning, not just decorate it.

## Built for

- plans before implementation
- diffs, PRs, specs, ADRs, and architecture notes
- finding brittle assumptions, intent drift, integration risk, edge cases, and silent regressions
- reviews that need more than a lazy "LGTM"

## What you get

- evidence-first critique
- one issue at a time
- one question at a time, with a recommended answer
- checks the repo, diff, tests, and specs before asking you to improvise
- clear concerns, why they matter, and what to do next
- sharp tone without losing review utility
